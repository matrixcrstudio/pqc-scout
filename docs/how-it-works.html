How it works

Every surface is scored on one question: given how long its data must stay confidential, is it already inside the window where a future quantum computer can decrypt what an adversary records today?

The Q-Day horizon model

Each algorithm family carries a nominal horizon — the years until a cryptographically relevant quantum computer credibly breaks it. The bands are grounded in published cryptanalysis, not guesswork:

  • NIST IR 8547 migration guidance and Mosca's theorem.
  • NSA/CISA CNSA 2.0 milestone timeline.
  • Gidney & Ekerå (2019) and Gidney (2025): the falling resource cost of factoring RSA-2048.
  • Cain, Bluvstein, Preskill et al. (2026): elliptic-curve discrete-log on P-256 in days on neutral-atom hardware — which is why 256-bit EC breaks first, ahead of RSA and finite-field Diffie-Hellman.

Harvest-now, decrypt-later tiering

An adversary recording ciphertext today only needs the earliest-credible break to arrive within the data's confidentiality window. PQC-Scout therefore tiers against a compressed horizon for asymmetric (Shor-class) primitives, following the Regev (2023) and Ragavan–Vaikuntanathan (CRYPTO 2024) line.

Grover is not Shor. The compression applies to asymmetric primitives only. Symmetric and hash primitives (AES, SHA, ChaCha) keep their nominal horizon — conflating the two would overstate the risk. This distinction is deliberate and load-bearing.

Exposure tiers

How a surface is classified
TierCondition
CRITICAL Earliest-credible horizon ≤ data retention: harvest-now-decrypt-later is live.
HIGH Nominal horizon ≤ retention plus a 3-year migration buffer.
MONITOR Adequate margin remains for a planned migration.
SAFE Already a NIST-standardized post-quantum scheme (ML-KEM, ML-DSA, SLH-DSA).

Sector retention priors

The tier turns on one number: how many years the data must stay confidential. A flat default mis-tiers the extremes, so a sector hint floors the horizon — and a floor can only raise severity, never lower it. The safe direction for a security tool.

The sharpest case is permanent human data. A genome never changes and implicates non-consenting relatives indefinitely; a biometric cannot be reissued. PQC-Scout models such data as an effectively unbounded horizon. Under that prior, every classical asymmetric surface protecting it is CRITICAL by construction — the honest consequence of the harvest-now-decrypt-later doctrine, not a tuning artifact.

Migration targets

Recommendations map each vulnerable primitive to its NIST-standardized replacement: ML-KEM-768 (FIPS 203) for key exchange, ML-DSA-65 (FIPS 204) for signatures, SLH-DSA (FIPS 205) for firmware signing, with hybrid X25519 + ML-KEM-768 recommended for the transition period.
